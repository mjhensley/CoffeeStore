Pour Over

The pour over method produces a clean, bright cup that highlights a coffee's unique characteristics. It's a meditative ritual that puts you in control of every variable.

Coffee 25g (about 5 tbsp)
Water 400g at 205¬∞F
Grind Medium-fine (like sea salt)
Time 3:00 - 3:30

Steps

  1. Rinse your filter with hot water to remove paper taste and preheat the dripper.
  2. Add ground coffee and create a small well in the center.
  3. Bloom: Pour 50g of water in a spiral motion. Wait 30-45 seconds.
  4. Pour slowly in concentric circles, keeping the water level consistent.
  5. Aim for a total brew time of 3:00-3:30.
  6. Remove the dripper once dripping slows. Enjoy!

Pro Tips

  • Use a gooseneck kettle for precise pouring control
  • Adjust grind size if brew time is too fast or slow
  • Fresh roasted coffee (within 2-4 weeks of roast) makes all the difference

Drip Coffee Maker

The automatic drip brewer is a coffee workhorse. With the right technique, you can make cafe-quality coffee with minimal effort.

Ratio 1:16 coffee to water
Example 60g coffee : 1000g water
Grind Medium (like coarse sand)
Time 4:00 - 6:00

Steps

  1. Use filtered water for the best taste.
  2. Rinse your paper filter before adding coffee.
  3. Add freshly ground coffee to the filter basket.
  4. Ensure your brewer reaches 195-205¬∞F for optimal extraction.
  5. Brew and serve immediately, or transfer to a thermal carafe.

Pro Tips

  • Clean your brewer monthly with a vinegar solution
  • Don't leave coffee on a hot plate ‚Äî it continues to cook
  • Invest in a brewer certified by the SCA for best results

Best beans for drip: Holler Mountain ‚Äî balanced, sweet, perfect everyday coffee

AeroPress

The AeroPress is a modern classic ‚Äî versatile, portable, and capable of producing everything from espresso-style shots to smooth, full-bodied cups.

Coffee 17g (about 3 tbsp)
Water 220g at 200¬∞F
Grind Fine-medium
Time 1:30 - 2:00

Steps (Inverted Method)

  1. Insert the plunger into the chamber about 1cm and flip upside down.
  2. Add ground coffee to the inverted chamber.
  3. Pour hot water and stir gently for 10 seconds.
  4. Let steep for 1 minute.
  5. Attach the filter cap with a rinsed paper filter.
  6. Flip onto your mug and press slowly for 20-30 seconds.

Pro Tips

  • Experiment with the standard vs inverted method
  • Try a metal filter for a fuller body
  • Perfect for travel ‚Äî it's virtually indestructible

French Press

The French press produces a rich, full-bodied cup with oils and fine particles that paper filters remove. It's simple, forgiving, and beloved worldwide.

Coffee 56g (about 8 tbsp)
Water 900g at 200¬∞F
Grind Coarse (like raw sugar)
Time 4:00

Steps

  1. Preheat the French press with hot water, then discard.
  2. Add coarsely ground coffee.
  3. Pour hot water evenly over the grounds.
  4. Place the lid on (don't plunge yet) and steep for 4 minutes.
  5. Press the plunger down slowly and steadily.
  6. Pour immediately to stop extraction.

Pro Tips

  • Use a coarser grind to reduce sediment
  • Don't squeeze the last drops ‚Äî they're bitter
  • Decant all coffee right away to prevent over-extraction

Best beans for French press: Trapper Creek Decaf ‚Äî smooth, chocolatey, full-bodied

Cold Brew

Cold brew is smooth, naturally sweet, and low in acidity. The long steep time extracts different compounds than hot brewing, creating a unique flavor profile.

Coffee 100g (about 1 cup)
Water 700g cold filtered
Grind Extra coarse
Time 12-24 hours

Steps

  1. Combine coarsely ground coffee and cold water in a large jar or pitcher.
  2. Stir to ensure all grounds are saturated.
  3. Cover and refrigerate for 12-24 hours.
  4. Strain through a fine-mesh sieve lined with cheesecloth or a paper filter.
  5. Dilute concentrate 1:1 with water or milk. Serve over ice.
  6. Store concentrate in the fridge for up to 2 weeks.

Pro Tips

  • Longer steep = stronger concentrate
