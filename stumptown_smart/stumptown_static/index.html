What Makes Coffee "Specialty Grade"?

Specialty coffee is graded 80 points or above by the Specialty Coffee Association (SCA). Less than 10% of the world's coffee qualifies. Here's what sets it apart:

100% Arabica Beans

Specialty coffee uses only Arabica, never Robusta. Arabica beans have more complex sugars and acids, producing nuanced flavors rather than just bitterness.

High Altitude Growing

Grown above 1,200 meters, often up to 2,000m+. Cooler temperatures slow cherry development, concentrating sugars and creating more complex flavor profiles.

Processing Methods

Washed, natural, or honey processed. Each method affects flavor ‚Äî washed for clarity, natural for fruit-forward sweetness, honey for balanced body.

Traceable Origins

We know the farm, region, altitude, variety, and processing for every lot. This traceability ensures quality and supports the farmers who grow exceptional coffee.

Shop by Roast Level

Roast level affects flavor, acidity, and body. Lighter roasts highlight origin character; darker roasts develop roasty, caramelized notes.

Light Roast

Bright acidity, floral and fruity notes. Origin flavors shine. Best for pour over and single origins from Ethiopia or Kenya.

Medium Roast

Balanced sweetness and body. Caramel, chocolate, and nut notes. Versatile for drip, pour over, and espresso.

Dark Roast

Bold, smoky, bittersweet. Low acidity, heavy body. Ideal for espresso, French press, and those who prefer intensity.

Single Origin vs Blends

Single Origin Coffee

Beans from one farm, region, or country. Showcases unique terroir ‚Äî the altitude, climate, soil, and variety that create distinct flavors. Best for experiencing a region's character.

Coffee Blends

Multiple origins combined for balance and consistency. We blend to create complexity ‚Äî one origin for sweetness, another for body, another for brightness. Ideal for espresso.
